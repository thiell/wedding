Biscarrosse

Staying in Biscarrosse is ideal for lovers of nature, water sports, and biking. Surrounded by forests, beaches, and lakes, this area offers a wide range of activities for all ages.

Around La Ferme d’En Chon

  • Camping En Chon les Pins and Camping Mayotte Vacances are inexpensive collections of cabins in walking distance to La Ferme d’En Chon that must be rented a week at a time. They have central swimming pools and limited food on site. Mayotte has lakeside access and lots of activities for kids.
  • There are a handful of vacation rentals on Airbnb or VRBO that are walking distance to the venue. These also require week-long rentals from Saturday to Saturday.
  • Many lakeside hotels, vacation rentals, and other “camping” accommodations are a short drive away.

Biscarrosse Ville

This is the commercial center of Biscarrosse, away from the tourists at the ocean and lakes. It is a good place to get groceries and find a few restaurants, but it is not particularly attractive. We recommend staying elsewhere.

Biscarrosse Plage

Biscarrosse Plage is a popular summer destination on the Atlantic coast. The beach there has impressive waves that attract surfers, water sports enthusiasts, and families alike. The town is smaller and feels a little more casual than Arcachon, and there is a walkable downtown area reminiscent of other beach towns with many restaurants, shops, and vacation rentals. It takes about 35 minutes to drive to Arcachon Town Hall from there and about 15 minutes to drive to La Ferme d’En Chon.

Arcachon and Around

Arcachon and Pyla-sur-Mer are popular seaside resort towns on Arcachon Bay. They are more cosmopolitan than Biscarrosse and very walkable, with lots of shops, restaurants, and cafes. The calm waters of the Bay are ideal for children. Dune du Pilat, the tallest sand dune in Europe and one of the most popular destinations in France, separates these towns from Biscarrosse.

Arcachon Ville d'Été

This is the downtown part of Arcachon, with many shops and cafes, a daily market, and close proximity to the calm waters of Arcachon Bay. If you stay here, you can walk to the town hall ceremony in the morning and then drive or hire a car to take you to the reception and back. The departure location for the Bay of Arcachon boat tour is also walkable from here.

We don’t have firsthand experience, but these hotels look promising:

Le Moulleau

This part of Arcachon is smaller and more upscale than Ville d’Été. It has a charming, sophisticated feel, with easy access to Arcachon Bay, an iconic church, and good restaurants. Staying here would require driving to both wedding events on July 13. It is about 10 minutes to Arcachon Town Hall and 40 minutes to La Ferme d’En Chon.

Pyla-sur-Mer

This town is full of beautiful homes in the traditional style of Arcachon and some of the best hotels and restaurants in the area. It’s worth driving through the area even if you don’t stay.

Bordeaux

Bordeaux is a charming city full of restaurants, cafes, shops, and parks. It’s known for its architectural and cultural heritage, and of course, wine. You can easily spend a few days here sitting in cafes and people watching, or you can use it as a base to take day trips or tours to other highlights of {% t titles.swfrance %}.

  • Stay in downtown Bordeaux, especially in or near the Old Town.
  • It is not recommended to rent a car unless you are traveling outside the city.
  • Convenient tram lines take you to stops within downtown, the Bordeaux Saint-Jean train station, and Bordeaux–Mérignac Airport.
  • Drive times are 70 minutes to La Ferme d’En Chon and 60 minutes to Arcachon, without traffic.
  • A train between Bordeaux Saint-Jean train station and Arcachon train station runs hourly and takes about an hour.
